Common questions about National Insurance Number

What is a National Insurance Number used for?+
Your National Insurance Number (often called NINO) is used by HMRC and employers to track your National Insurance contributions and tax payments. You need it for any paid work in the UK, claiming certain benefits, or accessing some government services. It stays with you for life.
Who needs to apply for a National Insurance Number?+
Anyone over 16 who plans to work, study with a work placement, or claim benefits in the UK needs a NINO. UK citizens usually get one automatically near their 16th birthday. If you never received yours or you're new to the UK, you'll need to apply through NI NO or directly with HMRC.
Can I start work without a National Insurance Number?+
Yes, you can start work without a NINO. Tell your employer you have applied or will apply. You may pay emergency tax until your number arrives, but you'll get a refund once HMRC processes your application. Don't delay applying — start the process as soon as possible with NI NO.
What documents do I need to apply for a NINO?+
You typically need a valid passport, Biometric Residence Permit (BRP), or UK birth certificate. Plus proof of your UK address like a utility bill, bank statement, or tenancy agreement from the last three months. NI NO can advise on your specific document needs.
How long does it take to receive my National Insurance Number?+
Usually 4 to 8 weeks from when HMRC receives your complete application. Some cases take longer if additional identity verification is needed or if you require an in-person interview. NI NO helps ensure your application is error-free to avoid unnecessary delays.
Will I need an interview for my National Insurance Number?+
Not always. Many applicants get their NINO without an interview. If HMRC cannot verify your identity digitally, they may ask you to attend an interview at a Jobcentre Plus. NI NO helps arrange these appointments and can provide translator support if needed.
What if I lost my National Insurance Number letter?+
If you've lost or forgotten your NINO, you can request a replacement confirmation letter from HMRC. NI NO offers a replacement service — we check your details and submit the request on your behalf. Your original number stays the same; you cannot get a new one.
Is NI NO a government service?+
No. NI NO is a private assistance service. We help you prepare, check, and submit your National Insurance Number application. You can also apply directly through gov.uk at no cost. We are not affiliated with HMRC, Jobcentre Plus, or any government body.
Can I apply if English is not my first language?+
Absolutely. Our online forms support over 70 languages so you can answer questions correctly without misunderstanding. We can also arrange translator support during any required Jobcentre interview. Just let us know your preferred language when you apply.
Do I need to print and post documents?+
No. You send digital copies (photos or scans) of your documents to NI NO. We handle printing, checking quality, and forwarding everything to HMRC on your behalf. One less thing to worry about, especially if you don't have a printer.
What happens if my application has errors?+
NI NO checks every application carefully before submission. If we find missing information, unclear documents, or mistakes, we contact you to correct them. This prevents HMRC from rejecting or delaying your application due to avoidable errors.
Can EU citizens apply for a National Insurance Number?+
Yes. EU citizens with settled status, pre-settled status, or those who have the right to work in the UK can apply. You'll need a valid passport from your EU country. NI NO helps EU nationals complete their NINO application correctly.
