First time applicant questions

Who can apply for a National Insurance Number for the first time?+
Anyone over 16 who has the right to work or study in the UK and has never been issued a NINO before. This includes UK citizens who never received their automatic NINO, visa holders, EU citizens with settled status, and new arrivals to the UK.
How long does first time NINO application take?+
Typically 4 to 8 weeks from when HMRC receives your complete application. NI NO helps ensure your application is error-free to avoid unnecessary delays. Some applicants receive their NINO sooner, some may take longer if additional verification is needed.
What documents do I need for first time application?+
You need a valid passport, Biometric Residence Permit (BRP), or UK birth certificate. Plus proof of your UK address such as a utility bill, bank statement, or tenancy agreement from the last three months. NI NO can advise on your specific situation.
Can I apply for NINO if I was born in the UK?+
UK citizens usually receive their NINO automatically near their 16th birthday. If you never received yours (perhaps due to address changes or parents not registering for child benefits), you can apply through NI NO as a first time applicant.
Do I need an interview for first time NINO?+
Not always. Many first time applicants receive their NINO without any interview. If HMRC cannot verify your identity digitally, you may be asked to attend an interview at a Jobcentre Plus. NI NO helps arrange this around your schedule.
Can I work without a National Insurance Number?+
Yes, you can start work without a NINO. Your employer will use a temporary reference number. However, you'll pay emergency tax (usually 20-40% instead of the correct rate). Apply as soon as possible and give your NINO to your employer once received to correct your tax code.
What if I never received my NINO letter?+
If you applied but never received your letter, it may have been lost in post or sent to an old address. NI NO can help request a replacement confirmation letter from HMRC with your existing number.
Can international students apply for first time NINO?+
Yes, international students with a Student visa that allows work (usually 20 hours per week during term) can apply for their first National Insurance Number. You'll need your passport, BRP, and proof of UK address like university accommodation letter or tenancy agreement.
What's the difference between first time application and replacement?+
First time application is for people who have never been issued a National Insurance Number. Replacement service is for people who already have a NINO but lost their letter or forgot their number. NI NO offers both services — choose the right one for your situation.
