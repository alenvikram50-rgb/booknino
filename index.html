Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know about your National Insurance Number

What is a National Insurance Number?
Your unique UK tax and contributions code used by HMRC and employers. You need it for any paid work in Britain.
Who can apply for an NI Number?
Anyone over 16 with right to work or study in the UK — UK citizens, international residents, visa holders.
How long does it take?
Usually 4 to 8 weeks after correct submission. We help make your application error-free to avoid delays.
What if I lost my NI Number letter?
We offer a replacement service to request an official HMRC confirmation letter with your existing number.
Do I need an interview?
Not always. If needed, we book your Jobcentre slot and prepare you for what to expect.
Is NI NO a government website?
No. We're a private assistance service. You can also apply for free directly via gov.uk.
Can I apply if English isn't my first language?
Absolutely. Our forms support 70+ languages and we arrange translator support for interviews.
Do I need to print anything?
No. Send digital copies — we handle printing, checking, and forwarding.
What documents do I need?
Valid passport, BRP, or UK birth certificate. We'll tell you exactly which based on your situation.
How do I contact support?
Email support@booknino.co.uk or enquiries@booknino.co.uk — we reply within 1-2 hours on weekdays.
