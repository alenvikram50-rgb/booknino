Common questions about lost National Insurance Number

What should I do if I lost my National Insurance Number?+
Don't worry — you can request a replacement confirmation letter from HMRC. NI NO can help you submit this request quickly and correctly. Your number stays the same; you just get a new letter with your existing NINO.
Can I get a new National Insurance Number if I lost mine?+
No, your National Insurance Number is unique and stays with you for life. You cannot get a new number. The only option is to request a confirmation letter with your existing number. NI NO helps with this replacement process.
How long does it take to get a replacement NINO letter?+
Typically 4 to 8 weeks from when HMRC receives your request. NI NO helps ensure your request is error-free to avoid delays. You'll receive the letter by post to your registered UK address.
What documents do I need to recover my lost NINO?+
You need your full name, date of birth, current UK address, and any previous addresses where you lived when your NINO was issued. You don't need to send identity documents for a standard replacement request, but providing address history helps HMRC locate your record.
Can NI NO find my lost National Insurance Number?+
NI NO helps you submit a request to HMRC for your official confirmation letter. We cannot see or access your number directly — only HMRC can issue the confirmation letter. We ensure your request is filled out correctly to improve chances of a quick response.
What if I changed address since getting my NINO?+
Provide both your current address and your previous address where you lived when you received your NINO. This helps HMRC locate your record. If you've moved multiple times, provide as much address history as you can remember.
Can I recover my NINO online without post?+
HMRC typically sends confirmation letters by post to your registered UK address. There is no online portal to instantly view your NINO. NI NO helps make the process smoother by checking your request before submission, but the final letter comes by post.
What if I only remember part of my NINO?+
That's fine. HMRC can locate your record using your full name, date of birth, and address history. Provide any partial information you remember — it may help, but it's not required for a replacement request.
Where else can I find my National Insurance Number?+
Check old payslips, P60, P45 forms, tax returns, or letters from HMRC. Your NINO may also be on your personal tax account online if you've registered with HMRC's online services.
