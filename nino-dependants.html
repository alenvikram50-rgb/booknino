How dependants apply for NINO

Step 1 — Arrive in the UK and receive your BRP: You cannot apply before arriving. Your Biometric Residence Permit must be collected first.

Step 2 — Gather required documents: Your passport (with visa vignette), BRP, proof of UK address, and evidence of relationship to the main visa holder (marriage certificate, birth certificate, or civil partnership document).

Step 3 — Submit application through NI NO: Complete our online form. We check everything for errors before submission to HMRC.

Step 4 — Receive your NINO letter: Typically 4-8 weeks. You can start work without it but will pay emergency tax until your number arrives.

Relationship evidence for dependants: If you're applying as a spouse or partner, you may need to provide marriage certificate or civil partnership document. For children, birth certificate showing parent names. NI NO can advise on your specific situation.

Dependant questions about National Insurance Number

Do dependants need a National Insurance Number?+
Yes, if a dependant plans to work in the UK, they need a NINO. This includes spouses, partners, and children over 16 on family visas or dependant visas. Without a NINO, you'll pay emergency tax on your earnings.
Can dependants on spouse visa apply for NINO?+
Yes, dependants on spouse, partner, or family visas can apply for a National Insurance Number once they are in the UK and have received their Biometric Residence Permit (BRP). You don't need to wait for your partner to get their NINO first.
What documents do dependants need?+
You'll need your valid passport with visa vignette, your Biometric Residence Permit (BRP), proof of your UK address (tenancy agreement, utility bill, or bank letter), and evidence of your relationship to the main visa holder such as marriage certificate, civil partnership document, or birth certificate for children.
Can children over 16 apply for NINO as dependants?+
Yes, children aged 16 or over who are dependants on a family visa can apply for their own National Insurance Number if they plan to work. They need their own passport, BRP, and proof of relationship to the main visa holder (birth certificate).
How long does it take for dependants to get NINO?+
Typically 4 to 8 weeks from submission. Most dependants receive their NINO within 6 weeks. NI NO helps ensure your application is error-free to avoid unnecessary delays from HMRC.
Can dependants work without a National Insurance Number?+
Yes, dependants can start work without a NINO. Your employer will use a temporary reference number. However, you'll pay emergency tax (usually 20-40% instead of the correct rate). Apply as soon as possible after arriving in the UK to avoid higher tax deductions.
Do dependants need an interview for NINO?+
Not always. Many dependants receive their NINO without an interview. If HMRC cannot verify your identity digitally, you may need to attend an interview at a Jobcentre Plus. NI NO helps arrange this around your schedule.
Can dependants apply for NINO before coming to UK?+
No, dependants must be in the UK and have received their Biometric Residence Permit (BRP) before applying for a National Insurance Number. The application requires your UK address and BRP details.
What if I don't have a marriage certificate or birth certificate?+
If you don't have original relationship documents, you may be able to provide alternative evidence such as a letter from the main visa holder's employer confirming family status, or official translations of original documents. NI NO can advise on your specific situation.
Can adult dependant parents apply for NINO?+
Adult dependants (parents, grandparents) on family visas can apply for a National Insurance Number if they have the right to work in the UK. They need their own passport, BRP, and proof of relationship to the main visa holder.
