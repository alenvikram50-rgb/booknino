For students: If you're planning to work part-time during your studies, full-time during holidays, or have a paid placement year — you need a National Insurance Number. Apply early to avoid delays in getting paid.

Part-time work during term

Many students work 10-20 hours per week alongside studies. You need a NINO so your employer deducts the correct tax. Without it, you'll pay emergency tax until your number arrives.

International students

If your student visa allows work (usually 20 hours/week during term), you must apply for a NINO. You'll need your passport, BRP, and proof of address. NI NO helps international students through every step.

Placement & sandwich years

Paid industrial placements or sandwich years count as employment. Your employer will ask for your NINO. Apply before your placement starts to ensure smooth onboarding.

Using your term address

You can use your university accommodation or rented house address for the application. Just make sure you can receive post there — HMRC sends your NINO letter by second-class mail.

How students can apply

Step 1 — Check your right to work: UK students can work from age 16. International students should check their visa conditions (usually 20 hours/week during term).

Step 2 — Gather your documents: Valid passport, BRP (if international), proof of UK address (tenancy agreement, bank statement, or university letter).

Step 3 — Apply through NI NO: Complete our online form in your preferred language. We check everything before submission.

Step 4 — Wait for your NINO: Typically 4-8 weeks. You can start work without it but will pay emergency tax until your number arrives.

Student questions about National Insurance Number

Do students need a National Insurance Number?+
Yes, if you plan to work part-time during term or full-time during holidays, you need a NINO. It's also required for paid placements, internships, and any work experience that pays you.
Can international students apply for a NINO?+
Yes, international students with the right to work (usually 20 hours per week during term) can apply. You'll need your passport, BRP, and proof of UK address. NI NO helps international students through every step.
What documents do students need to apply?+
Valid passport, BRP (for international students), proof of UK address like tenancy agreement, bank statement, or university accommodation letter. UK students can use their birth certificate.
How long does it take for students to get NINO?+
Typically 4 to 8 weeks. We recommend applying as soon as you know you'll be working — don't wait until you've started your job. Apply early to avoid emergency tax deductions.
Can I work without a National Insurance Number?+
Yes, you can start work without a NINO but you'll pay emergency tax (usually 20-40% of your wages). Apply immediately and give your number to your employer once received to get any overpaid tax back.
Do students on placement year need NI Number?+
Absolutely. Any paid placement or sandwich year work requires a NINO for tax and National Insurance contributions. Apply before your placement starts so you're ready on day one.
Will I need an interview as a student?+
Not always. If HMRC cannot verify your identity digitally, you may be asked to attend an interview at a Jobcentre Plus. NI NO helps arrange this around your study schedule — even during term time.
Can I apply for NINO from my university address?+
Yes, you can use your term-time address — university accommodation, shared house, or rented flat. Just make sure it's a valid UK address where you can receive post. HMRC will send your NINO letter there.
What if I'm an international student with a Student Visa?+
Your visa typically allows 20 hours work per week during term and full-time during holidays. You can apply for a NINO using your passport and BRP. NI NO has helped thousands of international students successfully apply.
How does NI NO help students specifically?+
We understand student schedules — term dates, exams, holidays. We handle document checking, appointment booking, and all communication with HMRC. You focus on your studies, we handle your NINO application.
