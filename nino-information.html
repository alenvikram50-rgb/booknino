What is a National Insurance Number?

Your National Insurance Number (NINO) is a unique personal identifier used by HMRC and the Department for Work and Pensions. It tracks your National Insurance contributions, tax payments, and any benefits you may receive.

  • Unique to you — no two people share the same number
  • Required for any paid work in the UK
  • Stays with you for your entire life
  • Cannot be changed or transferred to anyone else

Why You Need a National Insurance Number

If you live in the UK and plan to work, you need a NINO. Employers require it to deduct the correct tax and National Insurance from your wages.

  • All UK employers must ask for your NINO
  • Non-UK residents need one to work in the UK
  • Required for claiming certain benefits
  • Used by HMRC for all tax correspondence

Lost Your National Insurance Number?

If you've lost or forgotten your NINO, don't panic. You can request a replacement confirmation letter from HMRC. NI NO offers a replacement service to help you recover your number without hassle.

  • Your original number stays the same
  • Receive official HMRC confirmation letter
  • Typically takes 4-6 weeks to arrive

Change of Name or Address

Frequently asked questions

What is a National Insurance Number used for?+
Your NINO is used by HMRC and employers to track your National Insurance contributions and tax payments. You need it for any paid work in the UK, claiming benefits, and accessing certain government services.
How long does it take to get a National Insurance Number?+
New applications typically take 4 to 8 weeks from when HMRC receives your complete application. Replacement letters take 4 to 6 weeks. NI NO helps ensure your application is error-free to avoid delays.
Can I change my National Insurance Number?+
No, your NINO is unique and stays with you for life. You cannot request a new number. If you change your name or address, you can update your details with HMRC but your number remains the same.
What if I lost my National Insurance Number letter?+
You can request a replacement confirmation letter from HMRC. NI NO offers a replacement service to help you recover your lost NINO without hassle. Your original number stays the same.
Do I need an interview for National Insurance Number?+
Not always. Many applicants receive their NINO without an interview. If HMRC cannot verify your identity digitally, you may be asked to attend an interview at a Jobcentre Plus. NI NO helps arrange this around your schedule.
What documents do I need to apply for NINO?+
You need a valid passport, Biometric Residence Permit (BRP), or UK birth certificate. Plus proof of your UK address such as a utility bill, bank statement, or tenancy agreement from the last three months.
Can I work without a National Insurance Number?+
Yes, you can start work without a NINO. Your employer will use a temporary reference number. However, you'll pay emergency tax (usually 20-40% instead of the correct rate). Apply as soon as possible and give your NINO to your employer once received.
Can EU citizens apply for a National Insurance Number?+
Yes, EU citizens with settled status, pre-settled status, or those who have the right to work in the UK can apply. You'll need a valid passport from your EU country as identification.
How do I update my name or address with HMRC?+
You can submit a change of details through NI NO. For name changes, you'll need supporting documents like marriage certificate or deed poll. Address changes can be submitted online with proof of new address.
